The productivity leak

Collectors lose time when every call starts from zero.

Collection calls stall when collectors overtalk, argue, skip the direct payment ask, leave weak notes, or negotiate against themselves before the client has answered the first clear question. The result is wasted handle time, unclear outcomes, poor follow-up, and another collector having to rebuild the account story on the next call.

Call philosophy

Humane Does Not Mean Weak

Many debtors are not used to being treated humanely by a collector. That does not mean the collector should become passive. This debt collection call flow is respectful, structured, direct, and designed to keep clients talking long enough to clarify ability, resources, objections, refusal, and next steps.

How the flow works

A repeatable sequence for better collection call outcomes.

  1. Verify/contact according to company policy
  2. Avoid disconnect
  3. State amount due
  4. Ask: "Can you pay that today?"
  5. Ask: "How much can you pay today?"
  6. Ask: "When can you make a payment?"
  7. Use APT: Acknowledge, Pause, Transition
  8. Probe ability/resources
  9. Handle objections
  10. Acknowledge emotional cues
  11. Clarify refusal
  12. Close and document
